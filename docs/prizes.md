# Prizes

Two categories. You can submit one project for both.

## Most Useful

Built something a real person — you, your team, a friend — would actually use on Monday morning.

**Judged on:**
- **Real workflow problem.** Concrete, narrowly-scoped, the kind of thing that quietly costs people 30 minutes a day.
- **It works end-to-end.** No demo-ware. Bonus if you used it live during the hackathon.
- **Clear win.** The "before / after" should be obvious in 30 seconds.

**Good signs:**
- You can name the exact person who'd use this and why.
- It's small. One job, done well.
- The MCPs you picked are obvious in hindsight.

## Most Creative

Built something surprising, playful, or weird-in-a-good-way. We're looking for ideas we wouldn't have come up with.

**Judged on:**
- **Originality.** We haven't seen this exact thing before.
- **The model earns its keep.** Long context, multi-step reasoning, or multimodal inputs are doing real work — not just a thin wrapper.
- **The MCP combo is unexpected.** Stitching three services together to do one strange thing scores high.

**Good signs:**
- People laugh, lean in, or say "wait, what?" during the demo.
- The agent does something that would be tedious or impossible without it.
- You're proud of one specific moment in the loop.

## Submission

By end-of-event, share:

1. A short README (problem, what it does, demo gif/video).
2. A working CLI command we can run with your `.env` provided.
3. One paragraph on what you'd build next if you had another day.
